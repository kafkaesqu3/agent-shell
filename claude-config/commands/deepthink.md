## Reasoning protocol

Execute every layer before writing the final answer. Show your work.

**Layer 1 — Restate the real problem**
What is actually being asked? Strip away the surface framing. What decision is truly at stake? What would a wrong answer cost?

**Layer 2 — Map the solution space**
What are all the plausible approaches? Include the ones that seem wrong at first glance — sometimes they're right for non-obvious reasons. Don't prune early.

**Layer 3 — Stress-test each option**
For each approach: where does it break? What assumptions does it rely on? What happens if those assumptions are wrong? Which failure modes are recoverable and which aren't?

**Layer 4 — Identify the crux**
What is the single most important factor that determines the right answer? If you're uncertain about anything, what would change your conclusion most? Name the crux explicitly.

**Layer 5 — Check for blind spots**
What are you not considering? What would someone who disagrees with your current direction say? Is there a simpler answer you've been anchoring away from? Is there a harder answer you've been avoiding because it's inconvenient?

**Layer 6 — Commit**
Given everything above, what is the answer? State it directly. If it's a decision: name it. If it's a recommendation: make it. No hedging, no "it depends" without immediately resolving what it depends on and which way that resolves.

**Layer 7 — Reversal check**
Before finalizing: if the opposite of your conclusion were true, what would the evidence look like? Does any of the evidence you encountered actually look like that? If yes, explain why you're still confident. If no, proceed.

## Output

Show all seven layers. The final answer comes after the reversal check, clearly separated. It should be the most defensible position you can reach — not the most comfortable one.
